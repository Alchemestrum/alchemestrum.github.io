## Phase 1: High-Speed Blueprinting (Days 1-3)

Map the entire 1,000-page book into your brain via high-density video before opening the first page.

---

### Day 1: The Domain Blueprint

- **Task:** Watch Pete Zerger's CISSP Exam Cram (YouTube) at 1.25x speed
- **Focus:** Domain 1 (Risk Management) and Domain 5 (Identity & Access Management)
- **Goal:** These are the most heavily weighted managerial domains. Take notes only on concepts that feel foreign to an IT engineer: Business Impact Analysis, Risk Frameworks, etc.

**Progress (Day 1 complete):** Domains 1 and 5 video covered. Material was largely familiar coming from Sec+ and CySA+. Repeating the viewing for deeper retention before moving to Day 2.

---

### Day 2: Technical Calibration

- **Task:** Finish the second half of Zerger's video
- **Focus:** Domain 3 (Security Architecture), Domain 4 (Network Security), Domain 8 (Software Development)
- **Goal:** Domain 4 will be natural given your network background. Pay close attention to Domain 8 (SDLC, software testing). This is typically an IT operations blind spot.

---

### Day 3: The Managerial Shift

- **Task:** Watch Kelly Handerhan's "Why You Will Pass the CISSP" and Andrew Ramdayal's "50 CISSP Questions" (YouTube)
- **Focus:** Mindset transition
- **Goal:** Learn to spot the Engineer Trap. Practice choosing the option that fixes the corporate policy or protects human life, not the one that executes a command-line fix.

---

## Phase 2: Ruthless Book Extraction (Days 4-7)

Do not read cover to cover. Treat the 10th Edition like a dictionary targeted at your weaknesses.

---

### Day 4: Domain 1 & 2 Blitz (Risk & Asset Management)

- Open the 10th Edition and flip straight to the end-of-chapter review questions for Chapters 1-4
- Score 80% or higher: skip the chapter text entirely
- Score below 80%: skim only the sub-sections explaining your missed concepts

---

### Day 5: Domain 3 & 4 Blitz (Architecture & Infrastructure)

- Repeat the diagnostic test approach for the networking and crypto chapters
- Your background in hardware, software, and infosec means you can likely skip 70% of this text
- Save your energy for weaker domains

---

### Day 6: Domain 5 & 6 Blitz (IAM & Security Assessment)

- Drill Access Control models: RBAC, ABAC, MAC, DAC
- Focus on provisioning lifecycles and auditing procedures
- Understand these from a high-level oversight perspective, not a system administrator perspective

---

### Day 7: Domain 7 & 8 Blitz (Operations & Software Dev)

- Review Incident Response lifecycles, disaster recovery steps, and secure coding frameworks
- Connect Domain 7 operations concepts directly to SOC analyst interview preparation

---

### Week 1 Milestone Checklist

- [ ] Watched 100% of foundational video material (Zerger, Handerhan, Ramdayal)
- [ ] Completed end-of-chapter quizzes for all 8 domains
- [ ] Registered book online to unlock the free Sybex/Wiley test bank (2,000+ questions)

---

## Phase 3: The Data-Driven Drill (Days 8-11)

Use a test, analyze, pivot loop. Do not take random quizzes. Target blind spots systematically.

**The Rule:** For every question you miss, open the 10th Edition and read the exact page explaining that concept. Write the definition in a digital notebook.

---

### Day 8: Domain 1 & 5 Deep Strike (Risk & IAM)

- Generate a 100-question custom quiz from the online test bank: Domain 1 and Domain 5 only
- Miss a question: find the exact page in the book, write down the definition

---

### Day 9: Domain 3 & 4 Deep Strike (Architecture & Networks)

- Generate a 100-question quiz for Domains 3 and 4
- Watch for the Engineer Trap: your IT background will push you toward technical answers (implement a firewall rule) instead of the managerial answer (update the network security policy)
- Force yourself to choose the high-level solution

---

### Day 10: Domain 6 & 7 Deep Strike (Assessments & Operations)

- Generate a 100-question quiz for Domains 6 and 7
- Pay close attention to Domain 7: incident response steps, digital forensics, patch management
- These are the core skills that will appear directly in SOC analyst interviews

---

### Day 11: Domain 2 & 8 Deep Strike (Assets & Software Dev)

- Generate a 100-question quiz for Domains 2 and 8
- Domain 8 is the most frustrating domain for infrastructure veterans
- Focus: SAST vs. DAST testing, SDLC phases, secure coding frameworks

---

## Phase 4: Full-Length Combat Simulation (Days 12-14)

The real CISSP CAT exam is adaptive and ends between 125 and 175 questions. Practice the pacing.

---

### Day 12: Mock Exam 1 (Baseline)

- Quiet room, phone off, no interruptions
- Take a full 125-question mixed-domain practice exam from the test bank
- Goal: test reading speed and focus endurance, not perfection
- Target: 70% or higher as a baseline score

---

### Day 13: Wrong-Answer Autopsy

- Do not take any new quizzes today
- Spend the full session reviewing every question you got wrong on Day 12
- For each wrong answer: read the test bank explanation, then find the topic in the 10th Edition if the explanation does not click

---

### Day 14: Mock Exam 2

- Take a second full-length 125-question mixed exam
- Apply the managerial mindset aggressively on every question
- Target: 75-80%
