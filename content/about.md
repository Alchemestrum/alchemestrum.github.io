I've been a techie my whole life. The kind of person who was taking apart computers before they knew what a motherboard was and writing scripts just to see what would happen. Technology has always been less of a career path and more of a personality trait.

Somewhere along the way, cybersecurity grabbed me and didn't let go. Not because of the career prospects or the job market, but because it combines everything I already loved: problem solving, tinkering, breaking things to understand them, and that specific satisfaction of figuring out exactly how something works by figuring out how it fails.

These days I spend a lot of my free time on platforms like HackTheBox, TryHackMe, and LetsDefend. Not because I have to, but because I genuinely enjoy it. Working through an investigation, chasing an alert back to its root cause, or getting root on a CTF machine does not feel like studying. It feels like the most interesting puzzle I've ever found.

The homelab documented on this site is the same energy applied to real infrastructure. A full SOC stack built on actual hardware, generating real alerts from live machines, broken more than once, and rebuilt every time. Every post here is documentation of something I actually did, hit a wall on, figured out, and wrote down so the next version of me (or anyone else in the same position) doesn't have to start from zero.

I also play CTF competitions when time allows. Web, pwn, rev, crypto. Wherever the challenge takes me.
