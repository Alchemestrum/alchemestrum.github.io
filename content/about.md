I've been a technologist my whole life — the kind of person who was taking apart computers before they knew what a motherboard was and writing scripts just to see what would happen. Technology has always been less of a career path and more of a personality trait.

Somewhere along the way, cybersecurity grabbed me and didn't let go. Not because of the career prospects or the job market — but because it combines everything I already loved: problem solving, systems thinking, breaking things to understand them, and that specific satisfaction of figuring out exactly how something works by figuring out how it fails.

These days I spend a lot of my free time on platforms like HackTheBox, TryHackMe, and LetsDefend — not because I have to, but because I genuinely enjoy it. Working through an investigation, chasing an alert back to its root cause, or getting a foothold on a machine I wasn't supposed to be on — it doesn't feel like studying. It feels like the most interesting puzzle I've ever found.

The homelab documented on this site is the same energy applied to real infrastructure. A full SOC stack built on actual hardware, generating real alerts from live machines, broken more than once, and rebuilt every time. Every post here is documentation of something I actually did, hit a wall on, figured out, and wrote down so the next version of me — or anyone else in the same position — doesn't have to start from zero.

I also play CTF competitions when time allows. Web, pwn, rev, crypto — wherever the challenge takes me.
